# Field Notes from a Walled Research Program

*A 4-day pass (capping a ~year-long hunt) for a profitable retail edge on Polymarket's 5-minute crypto up/down markets. The verdict was a clean **WALL**: the market is efficient-on-knowledge (Grossman-Stiglitz), fee-taxed exactly where an edge would live, and quoted by a single fair-value market-maker that makes no exploitable mistakes — there is no deployable retail edge. That negative result is not the asset. The **method** that produced it — the discipline that killed dozens of seductive false positives, plus the handful of checks that would have saved a year if known on day one — is. What follows is the transferable residue: portable to a sibling trading project, or to any data-driven, adversarial, cost-bearing decision problem.*

---

## If you remember only 10 things

1. **Count the LOSERS, not the trades.** Below ~30 adverse events your CI is a mirage — declare INSUFFICIENT, never a pass.
2. **Test the RESIDUAL (outcome − the market's own price), never the outcome.** Predicting reality is worthless; the price already does it.
3. **Scout OPPONENT-first, not strategy-first.** Name the sharpest player and the axis that decides winners before you build anything.
4. **The cheapest go/no-go: regress the mid on a public fair-value reference.** High R² + sub-second requotes = a fair-value maker is home = the venue is WALLED. This one check would have saved a year.
5. **Deflate every p-value and Sharpe by the HONEST number of configs you tried** — across the whole program, not per experiment. A mined p=0.002 is the order statistic of N noise draws.
6. **Joint-control against the obvious priced variable FIRST.** If your coefficient collapses with the mid / sharp-line / own-move in the model, you're just slow. It killed more candidates than any other test.
7. **Run the cheapest test that could KILL the idea first, on free data, before any build or capital.** Never build the bot before the signal is confirmed.
8. **Run an independent SECOND MIND on every positive whose only job is to refute it.** The author is motivated to keep the result; the skeptic catches the look-ahead, confound, or best-of-N flaw.
9. **Charge the VERIFIED live cost on every leg.** Neutral/spread structures pay it twice; a margin that survives only optimistic costs is not a margin.
10. **Pre-register the LOCKED params and the SIGN before you look; promote ONLY on a fresh out-of-sample pass.** A better in-sample number after re-mining is overfit, not edge.

## 1. Opportunity discovery — scout the opponent first

**Pick the pond by who's in it, not by the strategy you want to run.** Strategy-first commits you to a venue regardless of the counterparty; if a fair-value bot lives there on the same data faster than you, every tactic loses on every axis (speed, capital, info, fee). *War-story:* a year was spent racing a fair-value MM on the 5-min book — "a Bolt on every axis: same Binance feed, same Φ model, faster, fee on our side." *Transfer:* on any new venue, name the sharpest participant and the axis that decides winners; commit only if that axis is patience / risk-tolerance / being-small — one you are not last on.

**Run the fair-value-maker detector as your first, free, decisive filter.** A maker enforcing the price is the one structure no slow retail account can out-price; detecting it is a minutes-long kill that pre-empts months of doomed work. *War-story:* the test — regress the mid on a public fair-value reference and time the requotes: R²<~0.6 + lagging quotes = OPEN; R²>~0.85 + sub-1s requote = MAKER HOME = WALLED. The 5-min market was R²=0.91, born-mature. A second cheap tell confirmed it: Up and Down books were exact mirrors (down = 1−up) in 75-81% of snapshots = ONE synthetic-book bot per coin, and every face we tested (vol-confidence, circuit-breaker, inventory-skew, directional) walled for a coherent reason. *Transfer:* before trading any quoted market, fit a cheap public fair-value model to its mid, time its requotes, and check whether the two book sides are one synthetic mirror; if it tracks tight and fast, abort.

**Set the base-rate prior from your own kill-list, and KILL-ON-PRIORS when the family is already walled with no structural difference.** Most "new" ideas are an old wall in fresh clothes. *War-story:* the Kalshi favorite-longshot pond was flagged as "same shape as our walled favorite-tail (difference = retail counterparty, not a fair-value MM)" and later died on the same loss-light + sharp-convergence disqualifiers; "every new directional idea was the efficient-on-knowledge wall in new clothes." *Transfer:* keep a written KILL-LIST of dead families and their reasons; classify each candidate against it first. A catalog of failures is a faster filter than another experiment.

**Once the wall is structural, hunt the neglected niche — the neglect itself is the inefficiency.** Point the search at where your sharpest opponent has the least incentive to be sharp: the sleepier instrument, the underdog tail, the awkward window, the rebate-only corner everyone ignores because it can't scale. An edge survives only where the smart money isn't bothering. *War-story:* the entire multi-coin build existed to test exactly this — do the sleepier alts (SOL/XRP/DOGE/BNB) lag the BTC majors enough to clear the fee? — and the top off-venue candidate (delta-neutral funding carry) is a capacity-constrained corner the pros largely skip. The hunting ground was chosen correctly even though these particular niches still walled. *Transfer:* enumerate the niches your strongest opponent rationally ignores (too small, too illiquid, too odd) and test those before anything liquid and crowded.

**Treat every published / named / "this makes 30%" strategy as dead-by-selection.** The literature is survivorship-inverted: ideas get documented because they stopped paying. *War-story:* carry, favorite-longshot bias, vol-selling, ICT/SMT — all published, all walled or compressed; an 8-agent literature sweep surfaced zero live angle. *Transfer:* reserve build-effort for your own observed anomalies and first-principles decompositions that nobody else has tested.

**Name the payer before touching data: "whose pocket does this come from, and why do they keep paying?"** A forced/biased/non-economic counterparty gives a persistence mechanism that predicts OOS survival; "another discretionary trader playing my game" is noise. *War-story:* funding carry has a real payer (over-levered longs) → survivable; the 5-min taker edge "had no payer but our own optimism" → walled. *Transfer:* write one sentence naming who is structurally forced or biased to pay you, and what stops arbitrageurs from fixing it. If you can't, don't test it.

**Classify the worst routine opponent as PREDATORY (profits from your specific trade — fatal) or COMPRESSIVE (only lowers your return — survivable); use a fill-toxicity markout to tell them apart.** Against predatory flow your average is a mirage — you're picked off precisely when wrong. *War-story:* maker-in-noise was predatory (informed flow, −0.365/$1); funding carry is compressive (delta-neutral, no one picks you off mid-trade). *Transfer:* before resting orders anywhere, replay simulated fills and measure their markout over 1s/5s/30s; a fill that marks against you and deepens is informed — walk.

**To escape a structural wall, change the PRODUCT (role, info, scale, instrument, venue, or fee-structure) — not the tactic.** No documented winner beat a fast public-data maker at its own game. *War-story:* Benter (private data + scale), Jane St / Optiver (market-MAKING, not prediction), Medallion (many tiny uncorrelated trades), Betfair scalpers (BECAME the bookmaker) — none out-priced a fast maker; the only retail candidate (delta-neutral funding carry) lives entirely OFF the walled venue. When the pivot is to a premium / be-the-house game, price it as short a catastrophe, name the payer, and benchmark net-of-tail yield against the risk-free alternative (a tokenized T-bill) — never against the headline gross yield. *Transfer:* when a venue is walled, stop iterating tactics; ask which dimension of the product you can change, or leave.

## 2. Statistical rigor — not fooling yourself

**Gate on the LOSER count, not the position count — below ~30 losers, INSUFFICIENT, never a pass.** For an asymmetric payoff the variance and bootstrap CI are driven by the rare adverse events; <30 losers gives an artificially tight (at 0 losers, degenerate) CI. *War-story:* favorite-tail's "+0.005 breakeven" had <30 losers and regressed to −0.0029 once losers grew; the over-round gate's +0.0065 was "4 extra losers flip it negative"; the rigor module hard-codes MIN_LOSS=30 and calls loss-light the project's "#1 self-deception." *Transfer:* on any venue with rare costly outcomes (defaults, liquidations, churn, fraud, stop-outs), pre-commit a minimum adverse-event count; calendar time and total trades are the wrong unit.

**Test whether your signal predicts the UNPRICED RESIDUAL (outcome − the market's price), not the outcome — and watch it decay toward the sharp horizon.** An efficient quote already embeds public knowledge; only what it misses is tradeable, and a residual that shrinks as resolution approaches is informational latency you can't harvest, not a crowd bias. *War-story:* BTC trend predicts the outcome at corr +0.4 but the residual corr is ~0; the corrected cross-token-flow object had residual corr +0.0002, CI [−0.031, +0.032]; the strongest-ever raw signal (+0.080 favorite residual at 24h) collapsed to +0.0015 at 1h. *Transfer:* never validate against the outcome — regress on outcome-minus-consensus-price, and measure the residual at several horizons toward resolution.

**Deflate every p-value and Sharpe by the honest N of configs you actually tried.** The expected max Sharpe of N zero-edge trials grows like √(2 ln N), so best-of-hundreds noise routinely produces a clean 2.5σ "signal." *War-story:* the B-filter's in-sample p=0.002 rose to 0.041 on fresh data and died at any honest Šidák N≥3; the gate applies p_def = 1−(1−p)^N with N≈200, and a planted +6% edge SURVIVES at N=1 but FAILS at N=200. *Transfer:* keep an append-only trials ledger across the whole program (every param, coin, threshold counts) and pay the multiplicity debt once, against the total N — not per experiment.

**Put the obvious priced benchmark into the SAME regression first — if your coefficient collapses, you're redundant.** The fastest, most decisive kill. *War-story:* the joint logistic falsified the B risk-filter (the alt's OWN 15s move gated strictly better than BTC's) and VALIDATED the over-round gate (over_round coef −0.358, z=−3.42, p=0.0006, while the favorite's own ask was insignificant at p=0.40). *Transfer:* before celebrating any predictor, control for the strongest already-known / public benchmark; if the partial effect vanishes, the benchmark already contains it (true for ML "my feature beats baseline" too).

**Resample whole correlated CLUSTERS (windows / days / episodes), and price the result on the EFFECTIVE sample size.** Correlated rows make naive i.i.d. CIs 3-10× too tight; the real information is the number of independent clusters. *War-story:* cross-coin outcome correlation was +0.61, giving Neff ≈ 1.45 effective coins out of 5; a "+$1.18/fill, 92%-win" headline was 13 fills bunched into ~4 adjacent windows (~40 min) = ONE regime, and DOGE's per-coin PnL flipped +7.73 → −3.08 between runs minutes apart. *Transfer:* bootstrap by cluster, report effective-n, and distrust any headline resting on a handful of adjacent correlated events.

**Demand cross-sectional SIGN-COHERENCE before believing a positive — a pooled +EV carried by 1 of N independent entities is a cherry-pick, not an edge.** Pooling across independent cross-sections (coins, assets, regimes) is only meaningful if the sign holds in most of them; a positive pool average driven by one outlier is the cross-sectional twin of best-of-N mining. *War-story:* favorite-tail's pooled "+0.005" had 4 of 6 coins NEGATIVE; the conditional-skew whiff was BTC-only with SOL/XRP reversed; the token-fear FOLLOW signal earned belief precisely because it was positive in ALL 6 coins (won−mid +0.052, cluster-p=0.008). *Transfer:* before trusting a pooled positive, break it by independent cross-section and require the sign to replicate in a clear majority; one entity carrying the pool = reject.

**When the payoff is negatively skewed, the mean-EV lies — use a skew/kurtosis-aware test (PSR).** A −100%-on-miss bet has a heavy left tail the sample mean understates. *War-story:* favorite-tail's return stream had skew −6.8; "PSR/deflation reveal the +0.005 breakeven as mildly negative" — a negative-skew short-vol bet that nets negative once the tail is priced. *Transfer:* judge any pennies-in-front-of-the-steamroller strategy (short vol, carry, favorite-backing, insurance) with a skew-aware statistic, not mean PnL.

**Before reading any conditional move as a "mechanism," control for the outcome (measure within-winners).** If the regressor tracks the eventual outcome, a pooled conditional average inherits a spurious outcome-mix (Simpson) component. *War-story:* the σ roll-off's headline "+1.73% mechanical re-rate" was ~81% an outcome-mix confound — corr(Δask, won)=+0.65 (winners' asks march to 1, losers' to 0); winners-only it collapsed to +0.003, CI spanning 0. *Transfer:* whenever a metric moves with a variable that also tracks the outcome (drift, engagement, recovery), stratify by outcome before claiming causality.

**Smell confounds with two cheap diagnostics: a real gate's edge RISES as it tightens, and statistical orthogonality is NOT additive EV.** A gate whose residual *falls* as you tighten is concentrating informed flow, not isolating mispricing; two independent factors that cut the SAME losers add nothing. *War-story:* the SMT panic-fade gate's residual went +0.025 → −0.001 → −0.040 → −0.113 as it tightened (corr(ask-drop, BTC 60s) = +0.48 — the "panic" was correct repricing); and over-round + spot-margin were both independent in a joint logistic yet stacking added Δ+0.0005 (P=0.52) because Jaccard was 0.45 — "11 of 14 margin-cut losers were already cut by over_round." *Transfer:* plot a gate's dose-response (it must rise), and before betting an ensemble helps, compute the overlap of cases each component fixes.

**Gating/filtering is DEFENSE, not alpha — a filter on a negative-base process can stop the bleeding but cannot manufacture positive expectancy.** The best surviving bucket is bounded by the base process's ceiling. *War-story:* per-window gating (volume, BTC-move, hour-of-day, all real signals) separated 18%-from-47%-win buckets, but the best bucket only reached EV −0.00 to −0.06, none positive. *Transfer:* if your raw strategy is net-negative, confirm the un-gated process has positive expectancy first — a smart filter only avoids the worst losers of a losing game.

## 3. Market microstructure & domain truths

**Treat a fee or spread as the deliberate PRICE the venue charges for the obvious edge — and check WHERE on the price scale it bites versus where your edge lives.** Frictions are often shaped, not flat, so a real edge can be taxed out precisely in the band where it concentrates. *War-story:* Polymarket's verified taker fee = 0.07·(1−p)/stake — ~0.2% at p=0.97 but ~3.5% at p≈0.5 — and was introduced explicitly to neutralize the latency arbitrage that had paid bots ~$40M/yr; the real ~1s latency edge (+0.006/$1/s) lived near mid-prices and got dwarfed exactly there. *Transfer:* pull the actual fee schedule from the live API, compute net-EV with the exact formula, overlay the cost curve on where your signal fires, and hunt the frictions' blind spots, not the headline arb.

**Providing resting liquidity is adverse-selected by construction (Glosten-Milgrom): book one-sidedness predicts CONTINUATION, never reversion.** Your bids fill when informed flow runs you over. *War-story:* when the book went one-sided during a spike, the forward mid CONTINUED +0.040 vs +0.005 baseline (8×), only 28% reverted; even in calm windows one-sidedness continued; the mid-band maker fill residual was −0.36. *Transfer:* before assuming a rebate strategy is free money, measure fill-conditional outcome — if a filled bid correlates negatively with the next move (it usually does), the rebate rarely covers adverse selection.

**Exits are outcome-CONDITIONAL: a resting take-profit is winner's-curse selected, and crossing to exit fires preferentially on losers — so prefer holding.** A profit target fills when you're right and goes unfilled when you're wrong, so fill-probability is negatively correlated with outcome. *War-story:* win|fill 0.98 vs win|nofill 0.95; every realizable rest netted −0.006..−0.011 vs hold −0.0051; "rest-else-cross-taker" was the WORST option, and "sell orders escape the fee" was refuted (fees are taker/maker, not buy/sell). *Transfer:* never credit an exit at face value — condition fill probability on the outcome; often the correct policy is rest-at-target-else-hold.

**Reverse-engineer the dominant maker's model and attack its INPUTS, not its OUTPUT.** You can't out-compute a correct model on shared data; the only seam is a single input it estimates worse than you. *War-story:* the 5-min MM fit Φ(ln(spot/strike)/(σ√t)) at R²=0.91, padding vol ~15% and tracking realized vol at corr 0.74; decomposing into time / strike / σ / spot showed time+strike unbeatable, σ self-priced, and the ONLY structural seam was the spot/settlement-feed mismatch (it prices off Binance but the market settles on Chainlink — see §5). *Transfer:* fit the maker's pricing function, identify which inputs it observes vs estimates, and hunt only where it pulls one string wrong.

## 4. Research process & working method

**Run the cheapest KILL-test first — on data you ALREADY have, before any build or capital.** Order candidate tests by cost-to-falsify, not by likelihood of confirming; most ideas die, so spending the minimum to reach a kill maximizes ideas-evaluated-per-effort, and many ideas die on a quick screen of existing data without ever justifying new infrastructure. *War-story:* a resolved-markets pull (1384 markets) caught a seductive false positive (+0.080 residual, 97.7% win, p=0.006) "for FREE in ~1h" via pre-committed gates; a 4-year NQ/ES replay killed the SMT idea "in minutes"; and before building a volume-heavy L2 collector, the circuit-breaker idea was screened on existing book_events (16M points) — one-sidedness predicted continuation (+0.040 vs +0.005) = adverse-selected = DEAD, so the expensive collector was NEVER built. The motto: never build the bot before the signal is confirmed. *Transfer:* for any idea, ask "what free dataset and single check would falsify this fastest?" and run that before writing infrastructure.

**Pre-register the LOCKED params and the SIGN before looking, forbid re-mining the same data, and promote only on a fresh OUT-OF-SAMPLE pass — never a better in-sample number.** Re-fitting a threshold chases noise; the in-sample p-value is worthless once you've tried many configs. *War-story:* two adaptive-config experiments "DIED out-of-sample" from re-fitting free thresholds; the promotion rule forbids "a per-coin / per-hour argmax or a re-mine." *Transfer:* before any backtest, write the exact params, direction, and the result that would kill the idea; test once on held-out data; treat same-data improvements as overfit until they survive untouched data.

**Make parameters adaptive the SAFE way: self-normalize ABSOLUTE-scale constants via a causal trailing percentile, PER ENTITY — but leave PROBABILITY-LEVEL and CLOCK constants FIXED.** The prohibition ("don't re-fit thresholds") has a constructive other half. A raw spread/size/move threshold in absolute units drifts out of regime; expressing it as a causal trailing percentile (e.g. "spread in the tightest 30% of this coin's last N") tracks the regime WITHOUT re-mining the outcome — and you gate on the ENTITY (per coin), never on the regime. But probability-level constants (ask≥0.95) and clock constants (tl=30s) are structural, not scale, so re-fitting them is just overfitting. *War-story:* walk-forward adaptive config scored −434 vs −88 for the best fixed config (re-fitting actively HURT), yet earlier experiments died OOS precisely because their absolute spread/size constants were stale; the fix was self-normalizing those via causal trailing percentile per coin while LOCKING the probability and clock constants. *Transfer:* split your params into scale (self-normalize causally, per entity) vs level/clock (lock); never argmax a free threshold on recent data.

**Monitor a live edge's decay with an adequately-powered rolling statistic (rolling-Wilson lower bound), never an underpowered by-thirds split.** An armed edge decays as the regime/opponent adapts, but a low-power decay test (splitting the sample into thirds) can't separate real rot from noise — it will either cry wolf or miss the decline. *War-story:* the project explicitly rejected the by-thirds split as underpowered in favor of a rolling-Wilson monitor; the same loss-light fragility that fooled the static backtests (4 extra losers flip the over-round gate) makes a coarse split useless live. *Transfer:* watch a deployed edge with a rolling confidence bound on enough adverse events to be powered, and pre-commit the lower bound at which you disarm.

**Define every guard/filter in terms a LIVE system could compute at decision time — never relative to full accumulated history.** A guard scaled to all-history-so-far silently tightens as data grows, admitting an ever-more-extreme cherry-picked tail. *War-story:* the nested strategy's exciting +0.02 EV (n~273) was a guard artifact; the corrected live-matching guard admitted ~5× more fills (n~1300) and every config regressed to ~0.00, net-negative. *Transfer:* trailing windows and fixed levels, never "rank within all data seen."

**Don't auto-tune to recent data — hold parameters FIXED, attack the regime instead, and always benchmark against an equally-FRESH baseline.** Last period's best setting is selected on that period's noise; the real driver is often a regime the parameter merely correlates with, and an apparent edge is frequently just a handicapped stale baseline. *War-story:* a nested "edge" of −0.06 vs −0.25 vanished at 30-min refit because the baseline jumped to −0.06 purely from fresher signals. *Transfer:* vary the cadence/lookback knobs and re-benchmark against an equally-fresh naive baseline; an edge that lives in one knob setting is a hyperparameter overfit.

**Run a mandatory independent adversarial pass — a "second mind" whose ONLY job is to refute — on every positive.** The builder is motivated to confirm; give skeptics distinct kill-lenses (look-ahead, priced-confound, loss-light/multiplicity, same-wall-in-disguise, data-artifact) and default to disbelief — majority-refute = kill. *War-story:* a second-mind caught that the rigor module's OWN first gate was dead code ("a DSR slider, a k=1 sin in disguise") and corrected it; another killed the σ-lag candidate despite a clean monotone dose-response and perm-p 0.003; the B-filter died to the own-momentum confound. *Transfer:* institutionalize a refutation step separate from discovery; make the reviewer's success metric "found the flaw," not "approved it."

**Build the rigor gate ONCE as shared tooling and route every candidate through that single object.** Re-implementing CIs, fee accounting, and bootstrap per experiment guarantees divergent bugs and a quietly-lowered bar. *War-story:* a tooling scan found the fee curve re-defined 4+ times, the Wilson LB in 3 implementations, and the clustered bootstrap hand-rolled 5+ times; the fix was one gate module + one fee authority, which then ported to the sibling project. *Transfer:* centralize costs + significance test + sample-size floor into one importable module; passing it is the definition of "a result." A correct primitive built once should never be re-bugged.

## 5. Data & validation infrastructure

**Run two independent capture paths whose failure modes don't overlap, and monitor DATA-liveness, not just connection-liveness.** A single "best" feed has correlated downtime and a single blind spot; the dangerous failure is the silent one where the primary looks healthy but emits nothing. *War-story:* the collector is deliberately HYBRID — a sub-second WebSocket feed plus a 1/s REST fallback kept specifically because the market WS has a documented "silent freeze" (stays PING-healthy but sends no data for hours); a watchdog force-reconnects on data-inactivity, and each source swallows its own exceptions so one outage never drops the whole snapshot. *Transfer:* pair a high-fidelity source with a cheap differently-failing one, alarm on data-staleness, and isolate sources so an outage degrades coverage instead of halting collection.

**Split validation into "does the signal EXIST" (cheap deep proxy data) vs "does it BEAT the live quote + cost" (only scarce live data answers) — and never let the cheap stage stand in for the expensive one.** Existence is a function of abundant data and kills the power/overfitting enemy; the tradable object (price, spread, fee, fill) lives only in scarce production data, which is exactly where the edge usually dies. *War-story:* the cross-asset lead-lag PASSED Stage-1 emphatically on 23GB of free spot (575k windows/coin, r≈+0.11–0.13, 100% sign-stable across 5.5 years and 35 robustness cells) — but that was the UPPER BOUND, not EV, because the alt quote already prices most of the move; Stage-2 needed the live token DB. *Transfer:* prove the relationship exists on whatever deep/free proxy you can get, then prove it survives real costs on production data — and budget the wait for stage two.

**Know the HARD CAP on your tradable object's history up front: the binding date is the LATEST launch (or shortest-lived / fastest-to-degrade field) among everything the decision requires.** Abundant proxy history creates a false sense of depth; power is bounded by the scarcest required field, and perishable data can't be back-filled. *War-story:* spot reaches 2017-2021, but the 5-min product launched 2026-02-12 and resolved-market odds vanish after close — so the only EV-capable record is the collector's own months-deep DB; for the six-coin spot panel, SOL's ~Aug-2020 listing was the binding date, not BTC's 2017. *Transfer:* identify the binding field up front, size your power and timeline to it, and start collecting the perishable data immediately.

**Confirm the exact SETTLEMENT / ground-truth source; when you must use a proxy, measure its disagreement on the cases that MATTER — and if the proxy choice flips your sign, the test is inconclusive.** A proxy "close on average" still mislabels exactly the marginal cases that decide a binary edge; a near-constant basis cancels in the bulk but flips the boundary outcomes. *War-story:* the market settles on auth-gated Chainlink but the captured proxy was Binance — a ~16 bps near-constant USDT/USD basis (mostly cancels) plus a ~5.3% boundary-flip rate; spot mislabels ~3.9% overall but 21-50% near the boundary; and the Binance-vs-Pyth label CHOICE flipped signs (eth −0.073→+0.123, sol −0.020→+0.100), a swing exceeding the |r|~0.02-0.07 signal. The rule became: report under BOTH labels, non-robust-to-label = don't promote, and build the real-source adapter as the durable fix. *Transfer:* treat the settlement source as a first-class fact — capture it, quantify the basis and boundary-flip rate vs your proxy, re-run under every defensible proxy, and build the true-source adapter.

**Audit the provenance and accuracy of every field you sign your signal with — a corrupt input zeros out every signal built on it and looks identical to "no edge."** *War-story:* Polymarket's `side` field is only ~59% accurate vs on-chain ground truth (vs Lee-Ready ~81% on real exchanges) — the root cause of why "every flow / CTAP signal came out ≈0": they were fed a coin flip. *Transfer:* before building on a trade-side / timestamp / best-bid field, validate it against ground truth; a null flow result might be a corrupted input, not an absent edge — distinguish the two before concluding.

**Verify your streaming/online feature computation is bit-for-bit identical to its batch backtest, and that every feature uses only through-t−1 information.** Recursive/causal state (EWMA, rolling betas) is where backtest-live skew hides, so a backtested edge can fail to reproduce live. *War-story:* the adaptive cross-asset model was replayed both ways and verified at corr=1.00000, mean|diff|=0.00017, with z_t strictly using betas through t−1 — that equivalence is what made the live update trustworthy. *Transfer:* implement one causal computation, replay it both ways, assert numerical equivalence, and run the corrupt-the-future test on every feature.

**Adversarially test the VALIDATION HARNESS itself — a gate bug fabricates false negatives and false positives as easily as the strategy does, and you trust it precisely because it's "the rigorous path."** *War-story:* the maker-in-noise "corner is empty / 0 fills" verdict was a GATE BUG — a toxicity gate read pre-entry flow at window-open where it's structurally None, dropping 3713/3716 candidates; removing it the cell populated with 2254 fills and printed −0.365 (a stronger kill). Separately, the candidate auditor "never CALLED the gate" until a second-mind caught it. *Transfer:* unit-test your evaluation pipeline with known-signal and known-null injections — a planted +6% edge must pass at N=1 and fail at N=200, a planted null must fail; a verdict you can't reproduce on synthetic ground truth is untrustworthy.

**Shard the physical store per entity, one schema, one resolver.** *War-story:* each coin gets its OWN `data/<coin>/live.db` ("a coin is a folder, not a column") so the tens-of-GB/day writers never contend, with a single path-authority module; because the partition key is the domain key, the per-window queries are unchanged. *Transfer:* partition the store per entity with an identical schema and one resolver — scale horizontally without rewriting queries.

**Retain disposable RAW aggressively; keep durable OUTCOME rows forever.** *War-story:* `RETAIN_DAYS=3` drops the huge reconstructable raw-event tables while the tiny, irreplaceable outcome rows are kept long-term. *Transfer:* put time-based retention on the bulky reproducible raw, never on the small ground-truth — disk pressure should cost you replay convenience, not history.

**Make every record SELF-LOCATING from a deterministic domain key — don't infer position from ingest order.** *War-story:* the slug IS the window-start timestamp, so each row carries its key + exact UTC + the raw payload verbatim and locates itself without trusting arrival order. *Transfer:* derive keys from the domain, store raw payloads verbatim, and stamp exact time — so a re-ingest or out-of-order write can't corrupt position.

**Name columns by CONTENT, and record an explicit trigger for any deferred rename.** *War-story:* legacy `btc_*` column names on per-coin DBs were paid down to neutral `price_*` BEFORE a cross-coin join could surface "btc_binance" on an ETH row. *Transfer:* name by content; if you must defer a rename, write down the exact condition (here, the first cross-entity join) that forces it.

## 6. Mindset & decision-making

**Treat the richest-looking flicker as the MOST suspect; pre-register sign + config + kill-criteria, and let losers accrue before deploying.** The strongest raw result in a noisy search is disproportionately the upper tail of noise. *War-story:* the single strongest result in the whole project (+0.08, 97.7% win, p=0.006) was a triple-flawed mirage (loss-light + convergence + look-ahead); the B-filter (p=0.002), peer-surge (+0.034), and spike-fade (12/18) all regressed to negative or insufficient as data grew — "the textbook deflated-Sharpe null asserting itself." *Transfer:* when a number looks spectacular, that is the cue to look harder, not to celebrate; graduate a candidate only at N adverse events with a deflated pass.

**Treat your own prior write-ups as optimistic by default and re-audit them adversarially.** Experiments are built by someone hoping the idea works, so they under-charge costs and over-credit favorable framings. *War-story:* the idea-audit concluded "the recorded verdicts were if anything TOO optimistic" — faster-feed taker was logged "breakeven" but charged zero entry fee (actually net-negative), and favorite-tail's "EXCL0" flags were a zero-variance artifact of 100%-win buckets, later moving to −0.0029, p=1.0. *Transfer:* periodically re-run your best documented results through the strictest current gate with full costs; assume the cheerful version overstated the edge.

**Under a binary / total-loss payoff, "many small consistent wins" is a trap — raising the hit-rate by lowering the target just lifts breakeven win-rate to meet you; the real lever is capping the LOSS.** When a miss is −100%, breakeven win-rate = 1/(1+ROI), so shrinking ROI to win more often raises the bar you must clear. *War-story:* DOWN@32c could hit 97% win at sell 0.33 yet still net ~0% EV; the real lever was a loss-stop, not a lower sell target. *Transfer:* when you want low-variance consistency in an all-or-nothing market, attack the loss tail (stop, sizing, selectivity); high hit-rate alone is an EV illusion.

**A clean, well-powered KILL is a real result — replicate it across independent cross-sections to upgrade "doesn't work here" into "structurally dead."** Cross-sectional replication removes the "maybe just this slice" escape hatch and lets you stop spending. *War-story:* the passive resting-limit strategy was killed per-coin across all 6 coins (pooled −0.31 EV/fill, CI [−0.46, −0.14], every coin's best config FAILS OOS) = "the strongest version of the verdict." *Transfer:* budget effort to kill ideas decisively (cross-section + OOS holdout + clustered CI), and treat a clean kill as success, not failure.

**The durable asset of a research program is the METHOD it teaches you — encode it as reusable skills / workflows, even (especially) when the program walls out.** A specific edge decays; the discipline that found and killed ideas transfers to every future project. *War-story:* after the WALL verdict the framing was "the wall is the SKILL of using data, not knowledge"; the response was two global skills (quant, data-detective) + three committed workflows (second-mind, vet-idea, scout-opportunity), made auto-invoking. *Transfer:* when a project ends — win or wall — extract the repeatable decision procedures (vetting, refutation, gating) into checklists/templates you carry forward. A negative result that hardens your method is a positive outcome for the next venture.

## 7. Knowledge management & archiving

**Archive KILLED and PARKED separately — each with a written verdict, the KIND of kill, and (for parked) a concrete REVISIT TRIGGER.** Two facts must survive every kill, because they look identical on net P&L but have opposite fixes: (a) was it ABSENCE (no gross residual — closed forever) or COST (real gross edge eaten by fee/spread — revivable by a cheaper entry); and (b) if cost-capped, the exact precondition (fee / spread / data threshold) that flips it live. *War-story:* `dead_ends/` (proven-dead, absence) is split from `ideas_old/` (real-but-capped, cost) — e.g. token-fear FOLLOW: won−mid +0.052, cluster-p=0.008, positive in all 6 coins at the mid, but −half-spread −3.1pp fee dropped it to +0.024, p=0.32 → filed "signal-real-at-mid, fee-capped," PARKED with "re-run when n_fired≳1800 OR if the 5-min fee drops," NOT filed dead. *Transfer:* tag each shelved idea DEAD-absence vs PARKED-cost, store the reproducing code, write the precondition that makes it live again, and re-check only when that trigger fires.

**Re-derive your decision inputs from the freshest data immediately before arming any live system, and stamp each acted-on record with the model version it came from.** Signals drift with the regime; a config valid hours ago can be wrong now, and version-stamping is what lets you score honestly later. *War-story:* the finder must be re-run before every executor launch "so it always trades the freshest signals — never a stale set"; a bug that reused a 1-minute-old set was fixed by removing the staleness skip. *Transfer:* wire a mandatory refresh/recompute step into the deploy path of any automated decision system, and tag every acted-on record with its model version.

## The loop

A repeatable cycle you can re-run on any new idea, in any domain:

1. **Frame** it as a factor with a one-paragraph trader-behavior STORY and a NAMED payer/opponent. *No mechanism + no loser = no test.*
2. **Prior** from your kill-list: which walled family is this? If nothing is structurally different (new opponent, new venue with no fair-value MM, a neglected/un-scalable niche, fee-free entry, a private signal) → **KILL-ON-PRIORS**.
3. **Cheapest kill-test first** — the single free/historical check that could falsify it fastest, on data you already have, before any build or capital. Ordered by cost-to-falsify, not likelihood of confirming.
4. **Gate** the survivor through the one shared rigor object: residual (outcome − price) net of the VERIFIED cost on every leg, ≥30 losers, cluster-bootstrap on effective-n, deflated by the honest program-wide N, joint-controlled against the obvious priced variable, sign-coherent across independent cross-sections, skew-aware if the payoff is asymmetric.
5. **Second-mind** — an independent skeptic whose only job is to refute it, with diverse kill-lenses. Majority-refute = kill. *A positive that hasn't survived an honest refutation attempt is not real.*
6. **Pre-register or kill** — if it survives, lock params + sign + kill-criteria (self-normalize scale constants causally, lock level/clock), promote ONLY on a fresh out-of-sample pass, and monitor live decay with a rolling-Wilson bound; otherwise archive it (DEAD-absence vs PARKED-cost, with a revisit trigger).

*The edge may not exist. The discipline that tells you so — cheaply, honestly, and once — is the thing worth keeping.*
